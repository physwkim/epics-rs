The C refactor eliminated `dbfl_type_rec` (a "virtual" field log carrying no data, requiring scan-lock + rset call to read the live record) by unifying live-record references into `dbfl_type_ref` with `dtor==NULL` to mean "not-yet-copied". base-rs has no `db_field_log` abstraction — there are no files matching `field_log`/`dbfl`/`DbFieldLog`/`FieldLog` under `crates/epics-base-rs/src`. Field reads (`field_io.rs`, `db_access.rs`) operate directly on `EpicsValue` which always owns its data (Vec<T> for arrays, owned scalars), and the producer copies are made under the record's `RwLock` read guard. The three-way dispatch and "lock-aware reference" hazard simply do not exist. A note for future work: should base-rs ever add a zero-copy field-log path for archive/streaming, the right invariants to encode are exactly those summarized in the analysis (a 2-variant enum: `Val(EpicsValue)` for inline scalars vs `Ref{ data, dtor: Option<...> }` distinguishing live-record borrow from owned copy). Closing as not-applicable for current code.
