The C bug is the aai (array analog input) record's pass-0/pass-1 device-support init split: `aaiRecord.c::init_record` only invoked `dprset->init_record` in pass 0, before linked records (e.g. compress/histogram/subArray) had allocated their `bptr`. The fix lets device support return `2` to request a pass-1 callback, after links are resolved.

`crates/epics-base-rs` has no aai record at all — the records dir contains `ai/ao/bi/bo/calc/calcout/compress/dfanout/fanout/histogram/longin/longout/mbbi/mbbo/sel/seq/sseq/string{in,out}/sub/transform/waveform/asub/asyn/scalcout/busy` but no `aai.rs`, no `aao.rs`, no `subarray`, no `lsi/lso`. There is also no two-pass `init_record` lifecycle: device support binding happens once during `IocApplication::run` after the database is fully built (see `server/ioc_app.rs` and `server/device_support.rs`). The whole pass-0/pass-1 race is structurally absent.
